Current Members

The C.H.I.L. current lab members.

Dylan Wood, PhD Student, 2016 - present

Dylan was awarded his Bachelor of Science degree in Physics from Austin Peay State University in 2014, and he subsequently began studies that fall at The Ohio State University (OSU) to pursue graduate work. He was awarded his Master's degree from OSU's department of mathematics in 2016 for thesis research on the numerical solution of convection-diffusion problems by implicit-explicit time-stepping coupled with the local discontinous Galerkin method. This work was in collaboration with the CHIL lab, and led to Dylan joining the lab and OSU's civil engineering department as a Ph.D student fall of that year. His current research is on development of a coupled storm surge/structural fragility model to accurately resolve flood defense system failure mechanisms in large-scale hydrodynamics simulations and accurately forecast such systems' performance during storm events.

Yilong Xiao, PhD Student, 2016 - present

Yilong is a Ph.D student from Singapore who received a M. S. in Civil Engineering from the Ohio State University in 2016. Having switched over from an academic background of environmental engineering and chemistry, he is currently focusing on improving numerical solution of the unsaturated flow problem in soil with the Local Discontinuous Galerkin formulation. In addition, he holds great interest in video editing and has produced quality products for the Department of Engineering Education.

Dominik Mattioli, MS Student, 2015 - Present

Dominik attended The Ohio State University for his undergraduate education and received a Bachelor’s of Science degree in Civil Engineering in 2015. Since then he has been continuing his education at OSU in pursuit of a Master’s Science degree in Civil Engineering and is expecting to graduate in summer 2017. With resources provided by the CHIL lab, Dominik has conducted his thesis research on techniques for automatically generating unstructured and high-quality meshes that utilize triangular, quadrilateral, and mixed-elements.
